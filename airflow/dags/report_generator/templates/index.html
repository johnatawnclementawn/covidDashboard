Visualization of COVID-19 in NYC

Cases Hospitalization Deaths

This dashboard visualizes the basic situation of COVID-19 in epidemic curves, maps, tables and bar plots in terms of daily, last 14 or 28 days. The most severe pandemic regions were identified in tables and maps to help government understand the basic pandemic trend, know how to allocate medical resources and adjust the prevention strategies.

The hotspots of confirmed cases and hospitalization concentrated mainly in Staten Island and Queens. Counties with lower incidence rate are Bronx and Manhattan.

Under the positive tested condition, people are more likely to get hospitalized and dead in the east and south edge of Queen county and northern Manhattan.

Data source: NYC Open Data, New York State Department of Health

Severe Morbidity Rate of Infestor

The darker colors are, the higher the severe morbidity regions have, which indicates that COVID infected people living in these darker regions are more likely to become severely ill due to lower vaccinated rate or other reasons. Therefore, the government needs to pay more attention to these neighborhoods, and more policies that encourage vaccination need to be formulated toward them.

Mortality Rate of Infestor

The darker colors are, the higher the mortality rate regions have, which indicates that COVID infected people living in these darker regions are more likely to dead from COVID-19 due to imperfect medical resource, poor hospitalization access or other reasons. Therefore, the government needs to provide more public support to these neighborhoods, and allocate more policy, financial and medical resource to these areas.
